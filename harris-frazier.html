Fifty Years at the Capitol

Harris Frazier Government Relations (HBS) has spent decades building relationships inside the Tennessee State Capitol — the kind that don't transfer with a press release. Originally founded in 1980, the firm is one of Nashville's most established government relations practices, now led by principals Estie Harris and Meagan Frazier.

Harris brings deep experience in the state budget process and health care policy; Frazier's practice spans health care, business, and transportation. Together they represent clients navigating everything from professional licensing to complex regulatory change — the same kind of institutional trust we needed their digital presence to reflect.

The Briefing

Every month, Harris Frazier's newsletter distills a session's worth of legislative activity — budget movement, regulatory shifts, licensing changes — into a briefing that clients and policymakers actually read. It's the same instinct that shapes their advocacy: translate something complicated into something a decision-maker can act on.

The Modernization

We treated Harris Frazier's digital presence the way they treat a legislative session — nothing improvised. Every channel, from the newsletter to the website, needed to read as carefully as the relationships the firm had already built.

A full digital rebuild: a revamped monthly newsletter, platform-native social content, and a website redesigned so a prospective client could understand the firm's services in a single visit.

The Modernization Framework

Harris Frazier needed a digital presence that could keep pace with a Capitol schedule without ever looking improvised. This is the four-step framework we used to modernize it.

01

Digital Audit

We audited the firm's existing website, social channels, and newsletter to see what was already resonating with clients and policymakers before recommending any changes.

02

Content & Social Strategy

We built a social media content calendar — engaging posts, graphics, and video — designed to keep the firm visible with stakeholders between legislative sessions.

03

Newsletter Overhaul

We revamped the monthly newsletter for clarity and engagement, making sure it led with the policy insights and client wins worth reading.

04

Website Redesign & Launch

We rebuilt the website into a modern, user-friendly platform that makes it easy for a prospective client to understand Harris Frazier's services — the redesign only launches once the story reads clearly.
