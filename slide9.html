A Credit Card Built On Conviction

Coign is America's first credit card built by conservatives, for conservatives. The Coign Visa card offers 1% cashback on every purchase, with one major difference from the cards it competes with: every dollar spent contributes to conservative causes and charities, instead of quietly funding the opposite.

The mission is simple to say and hard to build: a community of conservatives supporting conservative causes through everyday spending, making financial decisions that reflect their beliefs. Our job was to give that community a steady stream of content worth showing up for.

How We Built It

The Conservative Commerce Framework

Coign's audience needed content that felt like it belonged to their community, not a brand talking at them. This is the four-step framework we used to build a content engine that could keep pace with a growing movement.

01

Mission Audit

We started with the "why" — the causes, the community, and the belief that everyday spending should reflect personal values — before writing a single caption.

02

Voice & Visual System

We defined a consistent, value-driven visual language for graphics and short-form video that could carry the mission across every platform without feeling like an ad.

03

Content Production

Every week became a mix of scroll-stopping graphics, short-form video, and a newsletter that kept cardholders connected to the mission between posts.

04

Community-Driven Growth

Engagement and sign-up data decided what got amplified next — the loop only closes once the community responds.
